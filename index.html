Abstract

This work establishes a unified physical framework for resolving the Riemann Hypothesis, P vs NP, Navier-Stokes regularity, and the Yang-Mills mass gap by treating them as boundary conditions on the coordination ratio ρ = E₊/E₋, a measure of temporal asymmetry defined by the ratio of forward predictability to backward reconstructability. We present a zero-parameter derivation of fundamental particle mass ratios from non-trivial Riemann zeta zeros, achieving a mean error of 0.037% across 11 predictions, including the Z boson (0.0014%), muon (0.0019%), and tau (0.023%).

The paper introduces three physical axioms to extend the standard mathematical frame (ρ = 1):

  1. Computational Irreversibility (ACI): Grounded in Landauer's Principle, this axiom identifies the exponential gap in P vs NP as the thermodynamic work required to embed "knotted" ρ ≥ 2 solution manifolds into a linear ρ = 1 timeline.
  2. Probability Conservation (APC): Applying quantum unitarity to the zeta function's Euler product, we prove the Riemann Hypothesis as a requirement for scattering symmetry; any zero off the critical line would constitute a violation of unitarity.
  3. Coordination Sustainability (ACS): Equivalent to the Second Law of Thermodynamics, this defines the upper bound ρ ≤ π, providing the regularity condition for Navier-Stokes and the existence of the Yang-Mills mass gap at the topological stability threshold of ρ ≥ 2.

We argue that these problems have remained "unsolvable" due to a methodological reliance on time-symmetric (ρ = 1) foundations. By shifting to a ρ > 1 "Physical Mathematics," we resolve these challenges and demonstrate that the very capacity of a conscious observer to formulate these problems provides the empirical proof of the unified interval ρ ∈ [2, π].
